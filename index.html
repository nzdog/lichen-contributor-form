Workplace Transition Reflection

This is a space to reflect during organizational change. No pressure, no performance. Just a place to see what’s already true.

Why this matters: Amid change, it helps to name what hasn’t moved. Steadiness creates orientation.
Examples: “My sprint rhythm.” / “The product roadmap.” / “How I work with my immediate team.”
Why this matters: Change often starts subtly. Naming the early shifts helps you stay present and adaptive.
Examples: “I’m getting pulled into higher-level decisions.” / “Meetings feel more cautious.”
Why this matters: You’re already watching the field. Writing it down helps you work with the pattern, not just react to it.
Examples: “Leadership tone.” / “Slack energy.” / “Increased visibility on KPIs.”
Why this matters: Transitions amplify habits—helpful or otherwise. Awareness creates choice.
Examples: “I’m over-preparing.” / “Avoiding meetings.” / “Working late even when I don’t need to.”
Why this matters: You don’t have to hold everything alone. This names what might keep you supported and intact.
Examples: “A check-in with someone I trust.” / “Clearer direction about what not to worry about.”
Why this matters: Naming what’s unresolved helps protect signal. You don’t have to force clarity too soon.
Examples: “Whether I want to lead this project long-term.” / “How this shift fits into my own path.”
Why this matters: Clarity doesn’t always mean speed. You get to choose how you move through this.
Examples: “Protecting focus hours.” / “Speaking slower.” / “Waiting a beat before responding.”
